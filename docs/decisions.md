# Decisions Log

All decisions from the brainstorming sessions (2026-03-26), with status.

## Decided

| # | Decision | Rationale | Session |
|---|----------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | Brand name: **Prod or Pretend** | Alliteration, instantly understandable, memorable | 5 |
| 2 | Primary channel: **LinkedIn** (neckarshore.ai) | Existing presence, target audience lives there, algorithm rewards discussion | 1, 2 |
| 3 | Secondary channel: **X** (Prod or Pretend brand) | Looser tone, lower barrier, broader reach | 4, 5 |
| 4 | Target audience: **decision-makers**, not developers | Decision-makers buy services. Developers are the subject, not the audience. | 2 |
| 5 | Monetization: **acquisition channel** for consulting, not paywall | Without measurement, it's performance theater. Primary value is inbound leads. | 6 |
| 6 | Open source: **yes** | Credibility through transparency. If you critique others' code, yours must be visible. | 2, 6 |
| 7 | Tone: **constructive-critical**, never confrontational | Mirror reality, show the path forward. Solution-oriented leadership posture. | 1, 2, 5 |
| 8 | Interaction limits: **max 1/week/person, max 2-3 total** | Brand protection. Original poster should not realize what's behind it. | 2 |
| 9 | LinkedIn: **human-in-loop always** | Quality and brand protection matter more than speed. LinkedIn is the storefront. | 2, 4 |
| 10 | No direct commenting on original posts by default | Too risky, too intrusive. Own posts and reposts instead. | 2 |
| 11 | Dual-path analysis: **repo exists vs. no repo** | Absence of a repo is itself a finding, not a weakness of the product. | 4 |
| 12 | Tech stack: **TypeScript, PostgreSQL, GitHub, Playwright** | Consistent with OMNIXIS stack. No experiments at the infrastructure level. | 3 |
| 13 | Phase 1 approach: **automated detection, human QA** | No manual warm-up phase. MVP is technical from day one. Human reviews, not triggers. | 3 |
| 14 | GitHub org: **neckarshore-ai** | Separation of personal (GmanFooFoo) and professional. | 6 |
| 15 | Repo goes public quickly | Credibility requires transparency. Code is the storefront. | 6 |
| 16 | Channel rollout: **sequential**, not simultaneous | Learn before scaling. LinkedIn first, then X. | 5 |
| 17 | Platform-native content only | No external links in LinkedIn posts (algorithm penalizes). | 5 |
